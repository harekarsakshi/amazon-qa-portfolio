# Day 7
# Amazon Checkout Flow Test Plan

## What is this document?
This folder contains the **Functional Test Plan** for the Amazon Checkout Flow optimization project. It is written in simple, everyday language so that everyone on the team—developers, product managers, and new testers—can easily understand our testing goals.

## What does the Test Plan cover?
The test plan breaks down how we will test the 4 main steps a customer takes to buy items in their cart:
1. **Address Step** (Where the package goes)
2. **Payment Step** (How the customer pays)
3. **Review Step** (Checking final prices, taxes, and discounts)
4. **Place Order Step** (Clicking the final buy button and getting an order number)

## How is it organized?
The document is split into 10 simple sections:
* **Sections 1 & 2 (Objectives & Scope):** What we are testing and what we are purposely leaving out.
* **Sections 3 & 4 (Approach & Environment):** How we will test (manually and with automation) and where we will test (the private Stage website).
* **Section 5 (Schedule):** A simple Day 1 to Day 10 calendar for our 2-week work cycle (sprint).
* **Section 7 (Risks):** 5 things that could go wrong during our testing and exactly how we plan to fix them.
* **Sections 8 & 9 (Criteria & Bugs):** How we know when we are allowed to start/stop testing, and how we rank bugs (Blocker, Critical, Minor).

## Who is this for?
* **QA Testers:** Use this as your daily guide to know what scenarios to test and what deadlines to meet.
* **Developers:** Read this to understand how we find bugs and how fast we need them fixed (SLAs).
* **Product Managers:** Read this to verify that all business requirements are being safely checked.
